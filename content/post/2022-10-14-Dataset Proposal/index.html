Dataset 1 – US Colleges with leading number of international students

Link to the original data source: Opendoors

This dataset is from Opendoors, which describes US higher-education institutions with the top 40 leading international student population during the 2003-2021 academic years for five distinct institutional types (doctoral-granting universities, master’s colleges and universities, baccalaureate colleges, associate’s colleges, and special focus institutions). We downloaded the raw dataset in xlsx format. We chose the 2020-2021 academic year, did some basic processing, and converted it to a csv file. The processed dataset has 200 rows and 4 columns, with each row representing one institution and columns representing its name, city, state, and the number of enrolling international students, sorted by descending order of international students. We will be able to explore how the international student population is related to university ranking and geographic location among these “top-picked” US universities. We are able to load and clean data. This dataset size is limited. We will need to find other datasets that contain information specific to those 200 colleges and locations, We will address this by the small dataset size, by merging other datasets or more webscraping.

Dataset 2 - Rankings of the best American undergraduate programs

Link to the original data source: Kaggle

This dataset is from Kaggle, which describes rankings of the best universities for undergraduate programs in the US, containing information of 392 universities in 2022. The data was collected for Kaggle competition as a clustering problem by comparing tuition fees and enrollment numbers. We were able to download the dataset in csv format. In total, this dataset has 392 rows and 4 columns, with each row representing one institution and columns representing the US News ranking based on their undergraduate program, name, out-of-state tuition, and the number of students attending undergraduate college. For data cleaning, there are some universities which are missing data on tuition fees, so we need to remove or impute them when conducting analysis (generating plots, maps, running regression models). There is no information on the international student population, so we will need to find this data point from other sources. We are interested in adding a column of booleans to indicate whether the university is a public university or a private university. Within the current dataset, we are able to explore the relationship between the ranking of a university and its tuition fee. If we can merge the above-mentioned two columns (international population and private vs public), we will be able to further explore multiple factors that are correlated with international student enrollment.

Dataset 3 – US Colleges with highest percentage of international student body

Link to the original data source: USNews

This dataset is from USNews that describes 145 American universities in 2022 with top proportions of international students, which was collected for publishing consumer advice, rankings, and relevant analysis. We were able to do web scraping and generate the csv file for the dataset. Essentially, this data has 145 rows with 3 columns that represent the school name, location, and percentage of international students, sorted by descending percentage. We are able to load and clean this data to Rstudio. We are interested in adding a column that contains the total student population, which will help us compute the overall international student body population. We also are interested in finding their respective rankings. A main challenge is that while the schools may have high percentage of international students, they may not have a known ranking, which will be difficult to impute. We only scraped universities with at least 5% international student body, which limits the dataset size; however, we could scrape a few hundred more from the site if needed. Another challenge will be merging/joining the datasets.
